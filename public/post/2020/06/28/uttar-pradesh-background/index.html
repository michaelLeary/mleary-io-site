depicts the confluence of the Ganges and Yamuna rivers at Prayagraj, a pair of Matsya fish to represent the former hindu and muslim rulers of Oudh, and, a bow and arrow to represent the hindu deity Rama who was born in Ayodhya within the state.

3. India parliamentary representation

  • Original file size: 3818x4600 pixels and 75KB

  • PNG creator: The,Manish.

  • Wikipedia5 describes India’s legislative body as:

bicameral with 788 seats divided as to Rajya Sabha (Council of States) of 245 members, and, the Lok Sabha (House of the People) of 543 members all presided over by President of India;

Lok Sabha members directly elected by public who vote in single member districts.

Rajya Sabha members elected by State Legislative assembly by proportional representation.

  • For Uttar Pradesh there are 80 Lob Sabha seats and 31 Rajya Sabha seats representing 14% of total parliamentary seats.

6. Uttar Pradesh legislature

  • The state legislature is bicameral. The upper house of 100 members is called the legislative council or Uttar Pradesh Vidhan Parishad7.

  • Term for Vidhan Parishad members is 6 years. The 100 seats are drawn from five groups: legislative assembly, local authority, nominated members, graduate and teachers8. The distribution of seats is shown in table.

#>           Constituency Seats
#> 4 Legislative assembly    38
#> 3      Local Authority    36
#> 5    Nominated members    10
#> 1             Graduate     8
#> 2             Teachers     8

  • The lower house has 403 members and is called the legislative assembly or Vidhan Sabha9.

  • Term for Vidhan Sabha members is 5 years. Latest election was 2017. Upcoming election scheduled for 2022.

  • the BJP has 309 seats in the Vidhan Sabha and 319 seats for its Alliance representing share of 79% of total. The party with the next highest share of seats is that of Samajwadi Party with 49 seats or 12% share. Congress has 7 seats.
